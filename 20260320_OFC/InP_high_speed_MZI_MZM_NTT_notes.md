# High-Speed InP MZI / MZM Work, Especially NTT

Source basis: local OFC 2026 metadata in `ofc_browser/papers.json`, local extracted paper text in `extracted_text/`, the existing local synthesis `OFC_2026_high_speed_materials_papers.md`, plus web search and primary/reliable source pages checked July 2026. This note focuses on Mach-Zehnder-interferometer electro-optic modulators, with adjacent InP EML / EA-DFB work separated when it is useful for benchmarking.

## Executive Read

NTT's InP MZM line has two distinct but related thrusts:

1. **Coherent C/L-band IQ and coherent-driver modulators**: the Ogiso/Ozaki line using n-i-p-n heterostructures plus capacitively loaded traveling-wave electrodes (CL-TWE). This moved from >67 GHz EO bandwidth / 1.5 V Vpi in 2017 to ~80 GHz in 2019/2020, then to >100 GHz and <3.5 dB on-chip insertion loss for beyond-200-GBd twin-IQ PICs in OFC 2024.
2. **O-band datacenter/pluggable IM/DD MZ PICs**: the OFC 2025 postdeadline result is the cleanest current NTT datapoint: an uncooled 8-channel O-band InP MZ modulator PIC, 100-GHz-class bandwidth, net 3.2 Tb/s total, 400 Gb/s/lane PAM-6, 500 m, 20-80 C, about 5 mm square per NTT's topic page.

The technical pattern is consistent: use InP's QCSE-assisted phase shifter efficiency to keep Vpi low and device length compact, then spend most of the design effort on RF loss, impedance/velocity matching, packaging, and driver co-design. The "device" result alone is not enough; the best results are really modulator + driver + package/front-end demonstrations.

## Architecture Notes

| Theme | What NTT appears to be optimizing | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| Phase shifter stack | n-i-p-n heterostructure to reduce RF/electrical loss while retaining QCSE efficiency | InP's advantage is not just high EO coefficient; it is a compact, low-voltage phase shifter if RF loss is controlled |
| Electrode design | CL-TWE / optimized capacitance-loaded traveling-wave electrodes | Bandwidth comes from the RF transmission line as much as from the optical waveguide |
| Driver co-design | InP-HBT driver/AMUX, CMOS/SiGe drivers, FPC RF interface, flip-chip assemblies | The practical product boundary is modulator + driver + RF package, especially above 100 GBd |
| Coherent vs IM/DD | C/L-band IQ devices target >1 Tb/s/lambda coherent; O-band 8-lane PIC targets 400G/lane datacenter IM/DD | Same material platform, different optimization: coherent values IQ linearity/loss/PDL; IM/DD values lane density, temperature range, and O-band packaging |
| Temperature / TEC-less operation | OFC 2025 O-band PIC explicitly reports 20-80 C and no TEC | This is the part to scrutinize for datacenter modules: bias control, wavelength drift, ER/TDECQ over temperature, and packaging yield |
| Heterogeneous integration | Separate NTT membrane InGaAsP-on-Si work integrates DFBs, MZMs, SOAs, SSCs, and Si/SiN waveguides | Technically interesting for optical I/O, but not the same maturity path as the conventional InP coherent CDM product line |

## Practical Takeaways

- **NTT is probably the public leader in InP MZM bandwidth at low Vpi for coherent IQ PICs.** The >100 GHz / 1.5 V / <3.5 dB OFC 2024 twin-IQ result is a strong combination, not just a single headline number.
- **For 400G/lane O-band IM/DD, NTT's 2025 8-lane PIC is still the NTT MZM result to track.** In the local OFC 2026 cache, I do not find a new NTT MZI/MZM paper; NTT's 2026 high-speed hit is an adjacent EA-DFB/membrane result.
- **OFC 2026 adds three direct non-NTT InP MZM/MZI works that matter.** Fraunhofer HHI/MACOM shows the highest local InP-MZM IM/DD symbol-rate datapoint (`W3E.6`); Nokia shows a monolithic InP DFB/MZM/SOA transmitter PIC (`Th1C.4`); PETRA/Sumitomo shows InP/SOI bonded segmented IQ modulators for optical DAC (`M4D.2`).
- **Fraunhofer HHI is the closest public non-NTT InP MZM comparator for lane-rate scaling.** Their OFC 2026 co-packaged 76 GHz InP MZM + MACOM driver reaches 180 GBd PAM4 in O-band, but with heavy lab DSP/equalization and excluding laser/DSP/TEC power from the 1.45 pJ/bit number.
- **The main product questions are packaging and control, not basic material feasibility.** Bias control, thermal drift, RF launch/package loss, lane-to-lane skew/crosstalk, driver linearity, and wafer/module yield determine whether these are pluggable-ready.
- **Do not mix InP MZM results with InP EML results too casually.** EMLs dominate much of the 400G/lane O-band literature, but the MZM trade space is different: larger but potentially more linear, lower chirp, and more compatible with coherent-style driver/modulator co-design.

## Diligence Questions

1. What is the measured TDECQ / required equalization / FEC assumption for the OFC 2025 NTT 400G/lane PAM-6 result across 20-80 C?
2. Is the 8-channel O-band InP MZ PIC monolithically integrated with any source/SOA, or is it modulator-only with external lasers?
3. What are the per-lane insertion loss, Vpi or Vpp requirement, ER/OMA, and RF return loss across the eight lanes?
4. What is the bias-control scheme for uncooled field operation, and how many control loops are needed per lane?
5. What is the actual package form factor and RF interconnect approach for a 3.2T pluggable: wirebond, flip-chip, interposer, or FPC?
6. How does the cost/yield of an 8-lane InP MZM PIC compare with 8x EML, SiPh MZM, and TFLN engines at 3.2T?
7. For coherent CDMs, is NTT's C+L-band performance limited by modulator optical loss/PDL, driver bandwidth, RF package loss, or DSP/ADC-DAC front-end constraints?
